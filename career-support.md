# 🚀 SRE Career Support Bootcamp (4 Weeks)

## 🎯 Goal
Help participants transition into SRE roles by building:
- Strong SRE positioning
- Visible LinkedIn presence
- Active professional network
- Proof-of-work portfolio
- Interview readiness
- Ongoing job pipeline
  
---

# 📅 Week 1 — Positioning & Market Clarity

## 🎯 Outcome
Define clear SRE positioning and optimize LinkedIn + resume.

## Modules

### 1. SRE Career Positioning
- Understanding real SRE expectations
- Mapping current skills → SRE roles
- Defining:
  - Target roles (SRE / Platform / DevOps)
  - Target companies
  - Career narrative

### 2. LinkedIn Positioning & Personal Brand
- Headline frameworks
- About section storytelling
- Experience rewriting:
  - Reliability
  - Scale
  - Incidents
  - Automation

### 3. Resume Transformation for SRE
- Shift from tasks → impact
- Add:
  - SLIs / SLOs
  - Incident handling
  - Cost vs reliability decisions

## Deliverables
- Optimized LinkedIn profile
- SRE-focused resume
- Defined job target strategy

---

# 📅 Week 2 — Visibility & Credibility Building

## 🎯 Outcome
Become visible in the SRE ecosystem.

## Modules

### 1. Strategic Community Participation
- Identifying relevant communities
- Asking high-quality questions
- Answering to build authority
- Avoiding low-value participation

### 2. Building SRE Credibility Through Content
- Content types:
  - Learnings
  - Incident breakdowns
  - Tool deep-dives
- LinkedIn posting system:
  - 3 posts/week
  - Hooks + authority building

### 3. Personal Brand Flywheel
- Content → visibility → opportunities
- Positioning as an SRE practitioner

## Deliverables
- 3 LinkedIn posts published
- Community participation plan
- 2-week content calendar

---

# 📅 Week 3 — Proof of Work & Authority

## 🎯 Outcome
Build tangible SRE credibility assets.

## Modules

### 1. Proof-of-Work Portfolio
- What matters:
  - Observability setups
  - Incident simulations
  - Reliability improvements
- Project ideas:
  - Monitoring stack
  - Incident postmortem repo
  - Cost optimization case study

### 2. GitHub Portfolio Structuring
- Clean README storytelling
- Show:
  - Problem
  - Approach
  - Impact

### 3. Presenting Talks / Papers
- Converting learning → talks
- Submitting CFPs
- Speaking at meetups

### 4. Conference Exposure & Networking
- Identifying events and meetups
- Networking strategy:
  - Before event
  - During event
  - After event

## Deliverables
- 1 proof-of-work project
- Optimized GitHub profile
- Talk outline / CFP draft

---

# 📅 Week 4 — Job Strategy, Mock Interviews & Conversion

## 🎯 Outcome
Build a repeatable job pipeline and become interview-ready.

## Modules

### 1. Job Search Strategy for SRE Pivot
- Targeted applications (not mass apply)
- Role filtering:
  - SRE vs DevOps vs Platform
- Identifying relevant roles
- Application system:
  - 5–10 high-quality applications/week

---

### 2. Networking for Referrals
- Reaching out to:
  - Hiring managers
  - SRE engineers
- Message frameworks:
  - Warm outreach
  - Cold outreach
- Converting conversations → referrals

---

### 3. Mock Interview System (Core Module)

## Goal
Make candidates interview-ready through realistic SRE scenarios.

### A. Interview Structure Breakdown
- Linux / debugging rounds
- System design (reliability-focused)
- Incident handling scenarios
- Behavioral interviews

---

### B. Mock Interview Tracks

#### 1. Technical Debugging Mock
- Scenarios:
  - High CPU
  - Memory leak
  - Service down
- Evaluation:
  - Approach
  - Depth
  - Communication

---

#### 2. SRE System Design Mock
- Scenarios:
  - Highly available systems
  - Monitoring & alerting design
  - SLO-based architecture
- Focus:
  - Tradeoffs (cost vs reliability)
  - Failure handling
  - Observability

---

#### 3. Incident Simulation Mock
- Scenarios:
  - Production outage
  - Alert storm
  - Latency spike
- Skills:
  - Triage
  - Communication
  - Prioritization

---

#### 4. Behavioral Mock
- Questions:
  - Handling incidents
  - Failure scenarios
- Build:
  - Story bank (STAR format)

---

### C. Feedback Framework
Each mock includes:
- Strengths
- Gaps
- Improvement actions
- Re-attempt loop

---

### D. Mock Interview Cadence
- Minimum 2 mocks per participant
- Peer mock sessions
- Final confidence round

---

### 4. Interview Strategy
- Structuring answers clearly
- Handling unknown questions
- Driving the interview proactively

---

### 5. Alumni + Mentor Connect
- Mock interview partners
- Referral sharing network
- Weekly accountability

---

## Deliverables
- 2 completed mock interviews
- Personalized feedback report
- Story bank (5–7 strong answers)
- Interview readiness checklist
- 10 applications submitted
- 5 networking conversations initiated

---

# 🔁 Continuous Activities (All Weeks)
- LinkedIn posting (2–3/week)
- Community engagement (15–20 mins/day)
- Portfolio development
- Job applications (from Week 3)
